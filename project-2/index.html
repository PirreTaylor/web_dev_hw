How to Brew the Perfect Cup of Tea

By: Taylor Pirre

Tea is the magic key to the vault where my brain is kept.
- Frances Hardinge 

 Have your daily cups of tea been lacking
      that proper kick to them lately?
      In this guide, I will show you how
      to steep each leaf to its full
      potential!
    

 First, you will need to gather the
      necessary ingredients.
      This includes:
  • A teapot or heatproof mug.
  • An electric kettle.
  • Your choice of looseleaf tea.
  • Tea filters or a steeping device of your choice.
    • This can include anything from
      a plain [paper filter](https://www.amazon.com/T-Sac-Filter-Disposable-Infuser-Capacity/dp/B001BLCIN4) to everyone's favorite steeper, [the Manatea!](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00YAD1S0E/ref=asc_df_B00YAD1S0E5187200/?tag=hyprod-20&creative=395033&creativeASIN=B00YAD1S0E&linkCode=df0&hvadid=198075789534&hvpos=1o1&hvnetw=g&hvrand=8501217884889480063&hvpone=&hvptwo=&hvqmt=&hvdev=c&hvdvcmdl=&hvlocint=&hvlocphy=9021299&hvtargid=pla-321213676958)

     After gathering what you need,
      begin preparing the tea as
      demonstrated:

    1. Fill your electric kettle with water.
    2. Adjust kettle to heat water to desired temperature.
      • Note that differnt teas are best steeped at certain temperatures.
      • This includes around 200 degrees Farenheight for black tea, 175 degrees Farenheight for Green tea, and around 160 degrees Farenheight for white tea.
    3. While your water is heating, measure one teaspoon or more of your desired tea into your filter or strainer.
    4. Place filter or steeper in the vessel you will be steeping the tea in as the situation demands.
    5. When the water is done heating, pour it into your cup or teapot.
    6. Let your tea steep. The time needed to bring the tea leaf's flavor out [will vary depending on the type of tea you are brewing](http://www.divinitea.com/images/steeping-chart-full.jpg)
    7. Remove the teabag or steeper from cup/teapot and enjoy!
