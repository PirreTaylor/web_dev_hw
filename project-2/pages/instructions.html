Instructions: How to Brew the Perfect Cup of Tea


Recommended Water Temperature for Type of Tea:

Based on charts provided by Kramer at Market and chelletta.com

Tea Type Steep Time Temperature (Farenheight) Temperature (Celcius)
White 1-2 min 170 Degrees 80 Degrees
Green 1-2 min 170 Degrees 80 Degrees
Black 2-3 min 200-210 Degrees 90-100 Degrees
Oolong 2-3 min 180 Degrees 85 Degrees
Pu-erh (Chinese Aged Tea) 3-4 min 210 Degrees 100 Degrees
Rooibos (Red Bush Tea) 5-6 min 200 Degrees 90 Degrees
Herbal 5-6 min 200 Degrees 90 Degrees 

      After gathering what you need,
      begin preparing the tea as
      demonstrated:

  1. Fill your electric kettle with water.
  2. Adjust kettle to heat water to desired temperature.
    • Note that differnt teas are best steeped at certain temperatures.
    • This includes around 200 degrees Farenheight for black tea, 175 degrees Farenheight for Green tea, and around 160 degrees Farenheight for white tea.
  3. While your water is heating, measure one tsp or more of your desired tea into your filter or strainer.
  4. Place filter or steeper in the vessel you will be steeping the tea in as the situation demands.
  5. When the water is done heating, pour it into your cup or teapot.
  6. Let your tea steep. The time needed to bring the tea's flavor out will vary depending on the type of tea you are brewing.
  7. Remove the teabag or steeper from cup/teapot and enjoy!
